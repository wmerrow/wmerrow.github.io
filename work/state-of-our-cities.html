    Design Notes

    The State of Our Cities microsite aggregates education indicators from a range of sources in city-level reports, highlighting achievement gaps, revenue, teacher salary, and other issues.

    My work included aggregating data from over 100,000 schools across a range of indicators using R, and designing the visualizations and UX/UI.

    The scatterplot draws inspiration from this New York Times chart, using connecting lines to highlight the disparities between white students and other groups within a school district.

    The tool includes a range of chart types that all use a consistent design and color palette.

    In the ranking bar charts, shades of red are used for the primary city and comparison city, as an active highlight color separate from the demographic color palette, while gray and dark gray are used for the national average and state average.

    A visual "Explore" table allows power users to take a deeper dive into the data.
